Events of February 18th


Event 1

In 1930, American astronomer Clyde Tombaugh made the groundbreaking discovery of Pluto while working at the Lowell Observatory in Flagstaff, Arizona. This discovery was the result of meticulous work comparing photographic plates of the night sky to detect a faint, moving object beyond Neptune. At the time, Pluto was classified as the ninth planet in our solar system, a status it held until its reclassification as a "dwarf planet" by the International Astronomical Union in 2006. Tombaugh’s discovery remains a significant milestone in the field of astronomy.

Event 2

The first official air mail flight took place on February 18, 1911, in Allahabad, British India. This historic event marked a significant milestone in aviation and communication history. The flight, piloted by French aviator Henri Pequet, carried approximately 6,500 letters and postcards over a distance of 13 kilometers (about 8 miles) from Allahabad to Naini. Using a Humber-Sommer biplane, this endeavor demonstrated the potential of aviation for revolutionizing postal services, laying the groundwork for modern air mail systems worldwide.

Event 3

In 1519, Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés set sail for Mexico, initiating an expedition that would culminate in the fall of the Aztec Empire. Cortés departed from Cuba with a fleet of 11 ships, approximately 500 soldiers, 100 sailors, and a few hundred Indigenous allies and enslaved individuals. Upon landing on the Yucatán Peninsula, he encountered Indigenous peoples, including the Maya, and acquired crucial interpreters such as Malintzin (also known as La Malinche), who played a pivotal role in his negotiations and alliances.

Event 4

In 1943, members of the White Rose resistance group, led by siblings Hans and Sophie Scholl, were arrested by the Gestapo in Munich, Germany. This student-led movement opposed Nazi rule through nonviolent protests, distributing leaflets and painting anti-Nazi slogans. On February 18, Hans and Sophie were caught at the University of Munich and, along with Christoph Probst, were swiftly tried and executed on February 22. Their bravery has since become a powerful symbol of resistance against tyranny.

Event 5

In 1797, during the French Revolutionary Wars, British forces led by Sir Ralph Abercromby invaded Trinidad, which was then under Spanish control. The British fleet, consisting of warships and troops, arrived at the island and quickly overpowered the Spanish defenses. The Spanish governor, José María Chacón, surrendered without significant resistance, and the British formally took control of Trinidad on February 18. This successful invasion marked the beginning of British rule on the island, which was later formalized in the Treaty of Amiens in 1802.
